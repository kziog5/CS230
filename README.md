# CS230

Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
The Gaming Room has an application, Draw It or Lose It that is developed for Android systems. The game consists of four rounds of play, lasting a minute each and renders drawings at a steady rate. Teams then take turns guessing the puzzle that is presented to them. The client wants to expand their game to be playable on multiple platforms so they can reach a broader audience.

What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
For this project, I believe that my work highlights the strength and weaknesses of each platform and makes a good argument as to why Linux server is the optimal choice for the development of this game going forward.

What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Having a design document to follow throughout the development process helped me a lot. I was able to reference it and see what needs to be added and what was completed, which allowed me to work on each section specifically and not lose track.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
I believe that my evaluation section could be made stronger and a lot more information could be added about security and optimization considerations.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I interpreted the user’s needs in terms of ease-of-use and optimization. This ensures that the user will have a good experience with the game and they will want to return to the application, rather than abandoning it out of frustration. It is important to consider the user’s needs when designing an application because a good user experience leads to a successful application.

How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
I approached designing software by breaking down the project and fully understanding what I needed to do. I gathered all my information and documentation, and only then did I start writing code or developing a solution. The reason for that is that it allows me to work in a structured manner without having to be distracted by the need to gather information.
